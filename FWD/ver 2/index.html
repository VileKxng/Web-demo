About Us

Our Mission

To provide access to state-of-the-art equipment and quality care leading to healthy lives. We strive to improve the dialysis experience so that all our patients are comfortable and feel supported.

Our Vision

Nesta Carter saw the need for more dialysis options in Jamaica and worked hard on his vision to provide this lifesaving medical procedure. Mr. Carter partnered with Dr. David Edwards-Brennan to open First World Dialysis, a dialysis facility operated by a team of experts.

Our Values

Frequently Asked Questions

Dialysis is a treatment that filters waste and excess fluid from your blood when your kidneys can no longer perform this function.

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a term used to describe kidney damage or reduced kidney function (regardless of the cause) that persists for more than 3 months. Sometimes, CKD leads to kidney failure or end stage renal disease (ESRD), which requires dialysis or a kidney transplant to keep you alive.

Sometimes, CKD leads to kidney failure or end stage renal disease (ESRD), which requires dialysis or a kidney transplant to keep you alive.

If you have kidney disease, you should always consult with your doctor and dietitian in order to understand the best plan for you. However, people with kidney disease should eat healthy foods that contain antioxidants and low in sodium, potassium and phosphorus. Foods such as cauliflower, blueberries, garlic, cabbage, olive oil, red grapes, egg whites, fish, and high quality protein may be good options to incorporate into your diet.

You should ensure that you consult with your doctor and have adequate medication. First World Dialysis has a transient patient process to ensure that patients are safe and have a seamless transition whether visiting our facility or traveling from our facility.
